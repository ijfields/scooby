# Scooby — Marketing Plan

> **Version:** 0.1 (MVP Launch)
> **Last updated:** 2026-03-25

---

## 1. Positioning

**Tagline:** "Your stories deserve to be seen."

**One-liner:** Scooby turns your written stories into vertical drama videos — no editing skills required.

**Category:** AI-powered creative tool for writers

**Differentiator:** Unlike generic AI video tools, Scooby is built around the writer's workflow. It preserves the writer's voice, gives them editorial control through simple scene cards, and handles all the production automatically.

---

## 2. Target Audience (Marketing Segments)


| Segment                          | Who                                               | Where They Are                                              | What They Want                                                                 |
| -------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Primary: Unpublished writers** | Independent fiction writers with finished stories | Writing communities, NaNoWriMo, Wattpad, writing subreddits | To see their stories come to life, reach new audiences, potential monetization |

## 3. Pre-Launch Strategy

### 3.1 Waitlist & Early Access


| Action                     | Details                                                                                                     |
| -------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Landing page with waitlist | Simple page with value prop + email capture. "Be the first to turn your story into a vertical drama."       |
| Email sequence             | 3-email welcome series: (1) Welcome + vision, (2) Behind the scenes / how it works, (3) Early access invite |
| Referral incentive         | "Move up the waitlist by sharing" — each referral bumps priority                                            |
| Target                     | 500 waitlist signups before launch                                                                          |


### 3.2 Community Seeding


| Action                  | Details                                                                                                                                                                        |
| ----------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Writing subreddits      | Share the concept (not a sales pitch) in r/writing, r/selfpublish, r/screenwriting — "I'm building a tool for writers who want to create vertical dramas, would love feedback" |
| NaNoWriMo forums        | Engage during writing season — "What if you could turn your NaNoWriMo draft into a 90-second drama?"                                                                           |
| Writing Discord servers | Join and contribute value first, then introduce the platform                                                                                                                   |
| Writer Twitter/X        | Connect with indie authors, writing coaches, BookTok personalities                                                                                                             |


### 3.3 Demo Content


| Action                   | Details                                                                                           |
| ------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Create 3-5 demo episodes | Use the platform to generate sample vertical dramas from public domain stories or original shorts |
| Behind-the-scenes video  | Screen recording showing the full flow: paste story → edit scenes → generate → download           |
| Before/after             | "Here's a 500-word story. Here's the 90-second drama Scooby made from it."                        |

## 4. Launch Strategy

### 4.1 Launch Channels


| Channel                 | Approach                                                                                | Priority |
| ----------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | -------- |
| **BookTok / TikTok**    | Post demo episodes as native content. "I turned a short story into this in 10 minutes." | High     |
| **Writing communities** | Product Hunt-style launch post in writing subreddits and forums                         | High     |
| **Product Hunt**        | Launch with demo video and "made for writers" positioning                               | Medium   |
| **YouTube**             | Tutorial: "How I turned my short story into a vertical drama with AI"                   | Medium   |
| **Twitter/X**           | Thread: "I met an unpublished writer who..." (origin story)                             | Medium   |
| **Writing newsletters** | Pitch to writing-focused newsletters for features or sponsored mentions                 | Medium   |


### 4.2 Launch Messaging

**For writers:**

> "You've written the story. Now let the world see it. Scooby turns your words into a vertical drama video in minutes — no editing skills needed."

**For BookTok creators:**

> "Stop describing books. Start showing them. Turn any story into a 90-second drama for your feed."

**For writing teachers:**

> "Show your students what their stories could look like on screen. Scooby brings writing assignments to life."

### 4.3 Launch Week Tactics


| Day     | Action                                                            |
| ------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Day 1   | Product Hunt launch + announcement across all channels            |
| Day 2   | "How it works" behind-the-scenes video on TikTok/YouTube          |
| Day 3   | User spotlight — feature an early access writer's first episode   |
| Day 4   | Writing community Q&A / AMA                                       |
| Day 5   | "5 stories you can turn into dramas today" content piece          |
| Day 6-7 | Respond to feedback, iterate on messaging based on what resonates |

## 10. Risk Mitigation


| Risk                                              | Mitigation                                                                                |
| ------------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Writers don't trust AI with their stories         | Emphasize writer control, show editing step prominently, no story storage without consent |
| Generated video quality doesn't meet expectations | Set expectations clearly ("AI-assisted, not Hollywood"), show real examples               |
| Low completion rate                               | Simplify wizard, add save-and-resume, email reminders for incomplete episodes             |
| Content moderation issues                         | Basic safety checks on AI output, terms of service, report mechanism                      |
| Competitor launches similar tool                  | Move fast, build community, focus on writer-specific experience                           |
