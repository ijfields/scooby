## Executive Summary

Analyzed 5 competitor landing pages in the AI video creation space. Key findings:

- **Every competitor leads with a bold, action-oriented headline** above the fold with a free CTA
- **Social proof is universal** — logo bars, user counts, review ratings appear within the first scroll
- **"How it works" 3-step sections** are standard (Scooby already has this)
- **None target writers specifically** — this is Scooby's clearest differentiator
- **Enterprise positioning dominates** (Lumen5, Kapwing) — Scooby should lean into the indie/creator angle

### CTA Patterns

- **"Get started FREE"** or **"Sign up for free"** — every single competitor uses free-tier language
- **"No credit card required"** — Pictory adds this; reduces friction
- **Dual CTAs** — Lumen5 and Canva offer "Free signup" + "Request demo" / "Upload"
- **Embedded prompt input** — InVideo and Kapwing put a text input directly in the hero

### Trust Signal Types (ranked by frequency)

1. **Logo bars** (4/5) — enterprise client logos scrolling horizontally
2. **Review platform ratings** (3/5) — G2, Capterra, Feefo, Google with star counts
3. **User count claims** (3/5) — "20,000 companies", "4M companies", "30M creators"
4. **Named testimonials** (4/5) — real name + job title + company
5. **Compliance badges** (1/5) — SOC 2, GDPR, ISO (Pictory only)
6. **Press mentions** (1/5) — Variety (InVideo only)

### What None of Them Do (Scooby's Opportunity)

- **None target writers specifically** — they all target "marketers", "teams", "businesses", "creators"
- **None mention storytelling as the core workflow** — it's always "content" or "ideas" to video
- **None preserve editorial control** through a scene-card editing metaphor
- **None produce narrative-driven vertical drama** — they all make marketing/social content

### Headline Options to Test

Based on competitor patterns (action-oriented, outcome-focused):

1. **"Your stories deserve to be seen."** (current — emotional, writer-focused)
2. **"Turn your story into a vertical drama in minutes."** (outcome-focused, like Pictory)
3. **"Write it. Watch it. Share it."** (3-word rhythm, like Canva's punchy style)
4. **"From page to screen in 5 minutes."** (speed-focused, like Lumen5)

### Color Direction

- Current Scooby: monochrome with violet-to-indigo gradient (hero text only)
- **Competitors use:** clean white backgrounds with bold accent colors
- **Recommendation:** Strengthen violet/indigo as primary brand color, add a warm accent (amber/coral) for CTAs
- Avoid: pure blue (too generic), black-heavy themes (feels like dev tool, not creative tool)

### Trust Signals to Buicld (MVP Phase)

1. **Story counter** — "X stories transformed" (dynamic, grows over time)
2. **Speed claim** — "90 seconds of video in under 5 minutes"
3. **Writer-focused positioning** — "Built for writers, not video editors"
4. **Origin story** — "Born from a conversation with an unpublished writer" (authentic, memorable)

### Hero Animation Concept

- Scroll-triggered transformation: story text on screen dissolves/transitions into AI-generated video scenes
- Communicates the core value prop without reading any copy
- Technique: generate before/after images (story text screenshot -> finished scene preview), create a smooth transition video, embed as scroll-locked element
